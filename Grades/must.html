Grade 9

The Iliad

By Homer

Genre: Fantasy, Novel, Epic

A beautiful epic poem telling the heroic tale of the Trojan War. Filled with magic, jealously, blood, gore, raging gods and demigods, scheming goddesses and petty quarrels, "The Iliad" is a battle story. Events begin near the end of the decade long siege of Ilium (Troy), a battle sparked by the kidnapping of Helen. The men on both sides are exhausted and tensions between the leaders is rising and the gods are beginning to take sides and craft devious schemes. Who will win this battle?







The Odyssey

By Homer

Genre: Fantasy, Novel, Epic

The Trojan War has ended and all our heroes are heading home. For Oydesseus, the clever navigator, the journey home is about to become much more dangerous then he thought...Ten years have passed since the war ended, everyone is home...save Odysseus. At home, Odysseus's wife Penelope fends of suitors while their son, Telemachus, dispairs over his father's return. After a strange encounter, Telemachus sets off to find out what happened to Odysseus, unaware that elsewhere a god is taking an interest in his journey.





Hamlet

By William Shakespeare

Genre: Play, Tragedy

What are you to do when your father dies, your uncle marries your mother, becomes the new king, and may be responsible for your Dad's death? Hamlet, the prince of Denmark, is faced with just that situation. Ghosts, revenge, and madness abound in this tale of the tragedy of the Prince of Denmark.

To be or not to be?





As You Like It

By William Shakespeare

Genre: Play, Comedy, Gender-bender

Rosalind, left behind in Court when her Father flees after being disposed by his brother, lives under constant fear of banishment. When the sentence is suddenly passed, Rosalind, disguised as a young man, flees to the Forest of Arden in search of her father accompanied by her cousin, Celia, and the court jester, Touchstone. Unbeknownest to her, the young wrestler Orlando, with whom Rosalind has fallen in love, is also in the Forest. Hilarity insues.







The Old Man and the Sea

By Ernest Hemingway

Genre: Fiction, Novel

Told in language of great simplicity and power, it is the story of an old Cuban fisherman, down on his luck, and his supreme ordeal -- a relentless, agonizing battle with a giant marlin far out in the Gulf Stream.







Hound of the Baskervilles

By Sir Arthur Conan Doyle

Genre: Fiction, Novel, Thriller

Set largely on Dartmoor in Devon in England's West Country and tells the story of an attempted murder inspired by the legend of a fearsome, diabolical hound of supernatural origin. Sherlock Holmes and his companion Dr. Watson investigate the case.







A Separate Peace

By John Knowles

Genre: Fiction, Novel

Set at a boys' boarding school in New England during the early years of World War II, A Separate Peace is a harrowing and luminous parable of the dark side of adolescence. Gene is a lonely, introverted intellectual. Phineas is a handsome, taunting, daredevil athlete. What happens between the two friends one summer, like the war itself, banishes the innocence of these boys and their world.
