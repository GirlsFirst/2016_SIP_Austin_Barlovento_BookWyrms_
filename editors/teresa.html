Teresa Graves

Why, I’m just as true and honest as dirt. And I’m even more charming than dirt. ~Aly from "Trickster's Choice"

Age: 17 years

Home Town: Post Falls

HomeState: Idaho

Favorite Genre: Fantasy fiction

Favorite Author: I don't have a single favorite but these are my most read authors: Tamora Pierce, Diana Wayne Jones, Patricia C. Wrede, John Flanagan, and Shakespeare.

Where I like to read: I like to read on my bed or on the couch or on my window seat. Honestly, anywhere that I am not disturbed. I call these places my 'black holes.'

These are some of my favourite books that I would reccommend reading:

  1. Howl's Moving Castle by Diana Wayne Jones (and sequels)

    2. Has spunky and memorable main characters.

  2. The Chrestomanci Chronicles(series) by Diana Wayne Jones

    3. Magic, mystery, and alternate universes, what else do you need?

  3. The Ranger's Apprentice(series) by John Flanagan

    4. Medievel world with lovable characters and political intrigue.

  4. The Enchanted Forest Chronicles(series) by Patricia C. Wrede

    5. What would you do if you're a princess who hates doing 'princessy' things? This un-princess-like princess never expected this!

  5. Snow White and Rose Red by Patricia C. Wrede

    6. In this retelling of the classic fairy tale, new life is given to the story and characters. Fairies, magic, and...Witch Hunters!

  6. Trickster's Choice(duet) by Tamora Pierce

    7. Aly was raised to compete in the world of international espionage. What will happen when the Trickster god uncermoniously makes a deal (Did I have a choice?) with her?

  7. Wild Magic(quartet) by Tamora Pierce

    8. Daine has always had a way with animals. When her family was murdered by bandits, she left home. Along her journey she meets a horse trader and a hawk who...isn't a hawk?

  8. Posse of Princesses by Sherwood Smith

    9. Another strong female lead. Rhis, the unambitious princess, is invited to a ball...and ends up on a rescue misson?

  9. The Land of Elyon(quintet) by Patrick Carmen

    10. Alexa knows nothing outside the walls of the three cities. One day, she finds a passage outside and meets a very small man who seems to have been waiting for her.

  10. The Dalemark Quartet by Diana Wayne Jones

    11. The tale of the country of Dalemark in four parts spread across time and space.

  11. As You Like it by William Shakespeare

    12. Honestly, I have no way of accurately describing this play. All I can tell you is that it is very funny!

  12. The Count of Monte Cristo by Alexandre Dumas

    13. Dantes is a very clever man! I love how his web slowly becomes apparent to the reader and how you realize just how much he has controlled the action from behind the scenes.
